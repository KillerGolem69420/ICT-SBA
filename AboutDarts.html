The Game of Darts

About Darts

Darts is a popular pub game played by throwing small missiles (darts) at a circular target board, called a dartboard. It is a game of skill, precision, and strategy.

The objective of the game is to score points by hitting specific areas on the dartboard. The board is divided into numbered sections, with the center known as the bullseye. Players take turns throwing three darts each, aiming to score as many points as possible.

Darts can be played in various formats, such as 501, 301, or Cricket. Each format has its own rules and scoring system.

The Dartboard

DartBoard

The dartboard consists of different sections, each with a specific point value. The sections are divided into:

  • The outer ring, known as the double ring, which doubles the points scored in that section
  • The inner ring, known as the triple ring, which triples the points scored in that section
  • The bullseye, divided into an outer bull (25 points) and an inner bull (50 points)

Playing Darts

To play darts, follow these basic steps:

  1. Stand at a designated throwing line, usually called the oche.
  2. Hold the dart with a comfortable grip and align your dominant eye with the target.
  3. Aim at the desired section of the dartboard and throw the dart with a smooth motion.
  4. Score points based on where the dart lands on the dartboard, following the scoring rules of the chosen game format.
  5. Continue taking turns until a player reaches the winning score or completes the specified number of rounds.
